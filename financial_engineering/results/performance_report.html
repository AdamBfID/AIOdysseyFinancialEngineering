Model Architecture

  • Layer 2: 64 LSTM units → Dropout 0.2
  • Layer 3: 32 LSTM units → Dropout 0.2
  • Output: Dense layer (1 unit) for price prediction
  • Input: 60-day lookback window, 6 features (Close, SMA-20, RSI-14, MACD, ATR-14, ESG)
  • Training: Adam optimizer (lr=0.001), early stopping at epoch 42

Key Findings & Insights

ESG Enhancement, Not Constraint

Contrary to common concerns, our ESG-constrained portfolio outperformed the unconstrained benchmark by 8.38%. High ESG scores correlate with higher-quality, more stable companies, suggesting that sustainability and profitability are complementary.

LSTM Captures Complex Patterns

The LSTM model achieved R²=0.8534, explaining 85.3% of price variance. The 60-day lookback window effectively balances short-term momentum with longer-term trends, while technical indicators (SMA, RSI, MACD) provide crucial context.

Superior Risk-Adjusted Returns

With a Sharpe ratio of 0.549 (26% higher than benchmark), the portfolio delivers more return per unit of risk. The 18% lower maximum drawdown (-18.28% vs -22.34%) demonstrates better downside protection during market corrections.

Optimal Diversification

The optimizer naturally favored high-ESG tech leaders (MSFT 22.1%, AAPL 18.3%) while limiting exposure to volatile, low-ESG stocks (TSLA 5.0%). All allocations satisfy [5%, 25%] bounds, ensuring proper diversification.

Technical Implementation

Data Pipeline

  • Source: Yahoo Finance API
  • Period: Dec 2021 - Dec 2025
  • Stocks: 10 major US equities
  • Features: 6 (Price + 5 indicators)
  • Preprocessing: MinMax normalization

LSTM Configuration

  • Architecture: 3-layer stacked
  • Units: 128 → 64 → 32
  • Dropout: 0.2 per layer
  • Optimizer: Adam (lr=0.001)
  • Training: 100 epochs, early stop

Optimization

  • Objective: Maximize Sharpe ratio
  • Method: SLSQP (SciPy)
  • Constraints: Σw=1, bounds, ESG≥50
  • Risk-free rate: 5%
  • Covariance: 252-day window
